Stories, poems, tall tales about the rivers we fish.

I knew I was getting ahead of myself when I began writing the story before I had even caught the fish.

Following rivers throughout the West I kept finding stories eddying through my mind, the thought of a single trout rising unfolded into poem. Sometimes inspired by one-liners from my wiser than time fishing buddy Blake, sometimes just watching mayflies hatch from the water, these inner narratives took on a life of their own.

If you're thinking, I'm just a trout bum who gets skunked most of the time, you'd be right. But out there, when the current is running through core, when you're gazing up at the canyon carved by the wind over millennia, it's hard not to get lost in stories.

Fly-Fiction is my personal attempt to document that feeling. It's casting for that rainbow trout that you know is lurking under the alders. It's unclasping your fly box to pick out that size 16 elk hair caddis right as the sun drops below the rim. Here's my portrait of trout fishing my collection of tributaries I carry with me, here's fly-fiction.
